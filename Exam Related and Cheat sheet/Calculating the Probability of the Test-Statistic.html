Calculating the Probability of the Test-Statistic

Jeffrey M. Pisklak

  • Critical Region (=.05=.05) is shown in blue.
  • Solid vertical red line is the test-statistic.
  • The probability of a test-statistic (i.e. p-value) is shown in red.
  • Degrees of freedom are set to 4 in all examples.


Two-Tailed Tests

1. If your test-statistic is negative (e.g., T=1.5T=1.5), then you are going to use . . .

pt(-1.5, df = 4) * 2
## [1] 0.208


2. If your test-statistic is positive (e.g., T=1.5T=1.5), then you are going to use . . .

pt(1.5, df = 4, lower.tail = FALSE) * 2
## [1] 0.208


One-tailed tests

3. If your critical region is in the left tail (i.e., H0:>xH0:>x) then you are going to use . . .

pt(1.5, df = 4)
## [1] 0.896


4. If your critical region is in the right tail (i.e., H0:<xH0:<x) then you are going to use . . .

pt(1.5, df = 4, lower.tail = FALSE)
## [1] 0.104
